Mt. Fuji
Mount Fuji is Japan's tallest mountain, standing at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). It is an iconic, snow-capped volcano known for its symmetrical cone shape and cultural significance. Located on Honshu Island near Tokyo, Mount Fuji is a popular destination for climbers, photographers, and spiritual seekers. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Wat Phra That Doi Suthep
Wat Phra That Doi Suthep is a sacred Buddhist temple located on Doi Suthep mountain, about 15 kilometers from Chiang Mai, Thailand. It was built in the 14th century and is one of the most important and respected temples in northern Thailand. The temple is famous for its beautiful golden stupa (also called a chedi), which is believed to hold a relic of the Buddha.
Golden Gate Bridge
Golden Gate Bridge is a famous suspension bridge in San Francisco, California, that connects the city to Marin County. It was completed in 1937 and is known for its tall towers, long span, and iconic orange-red color. The bridge crosses the Golden Gate Strait, the entrance to San Francisco Bay from the Pacific Ocean, and is considered one of the most beautiful and photographed bridges in the world.
Sydney Opera House
Sydney Opera House is a world-famous performing arts center located in Sydney, Australia. It was opened in 1973 and is known for its unique sail-like design. The Opera House hosts concerts, theater, opera, and other events, and is one of the most iconic landmarks in Australia.
Bora Bora
Bora Bora is a small island in the South Pacific, part of French Polynesia. It is known for its clear blue waters, overwater bungalows, white sandy beaches, and a large central volcano called Mount Otemanu. Bora Bora is a popular destination for honeymoons and luxury vacations.
